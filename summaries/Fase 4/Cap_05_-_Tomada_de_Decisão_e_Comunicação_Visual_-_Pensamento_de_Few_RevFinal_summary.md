# Cap 05 - Tomada de Decisão e Comunicação Visual - Pensamento de Few RevFinal.pdf

**File type:** PDF | **Processed:** 2026-04-22

## TL;DR
A deep dive into why your brain sucks at making 35,000 decisions a day and how visual communication (Stephen Few style) acts as a shortcut to clarity.

## What's Inside
- **The 10-Second Gap:** Neuroscientific proof that your brain often makes a choice before you are consciously aware of it.
- **Decision Fatigue:** How the "reptilian brain" triggers fight, flight, or freeze when overloaded—explaining why picking a Netflix movie after work feels impossible.
- **Batching Strategy:** Why processing information "in batches" saves massive cognitive energy compared to handling fragmented, constant updates.
- **Visual Evolution:** A timeline of data "firsts," from 6,200 BC maps to William Playfair's 1801 invention of the pie chart.
- **The Quality Trap:** Research showing that more data often leads to *worse* decisions; visual quality always beats spreadsheet volume.

## Worth Knowing
The brain processes images significantly faster than text because it leverages ancient survival instincts—meaning a well-designed dashboard literally requires less physical "work" for the brain to process than a list of numbers.
