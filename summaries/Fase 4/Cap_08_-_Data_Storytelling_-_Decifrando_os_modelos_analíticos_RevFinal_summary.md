# Cap 08 - Data Storytelling - Decifrando os modelos analíticos RevFinal.pdf

**File type:** PDF | **Processed:** 2026-04-22

## TL;DR
A practical guide on using the Storytelling Canvas and specific chart types to transform raw data into persuasive, goal-oriented narratives.

## What's Inside
- **Storytelling Canvas:** A strategic roadmap for data exploration, building narrative arguments, and tailoring the delivery to specific audience personas.
- **The "Three Questions" Framework:** A shortcut for defining clear objectives by identifying the *What*, *Why*, and *Who*.
- **Temporal Progress Patterns:** Best practices for line charts, including the use of horizontal goal lines and historical comparisons to provide immediate context.
- **Visual Hierarchy:** Tactical advice on using 100% stacked bar charts and specific formatting to highlight trends or prioritize information.

## Worth Knowing
In this framework, "characters" aren't people—they are the sequence of visual elements (dashboards, slides, or charts) that lead the audience through the story. The guide also suggests that keeping full digits (e.g., $50,000 instead of $50k) can sometimes be more effective when you want the specific magnitude of a number to feel more "heavy" and impactful.
